Based on the symptoms you've provided, the first aid treatment is as follows:

Step 1. Avoid Movement: Try not to move the affected joint to prevent further injury.

Step 2. Immobilize: Splint the joint in its current position to prevent any further displacement.

Step 3. Apply Ice: Use ice packs wrapped in a cloth to reduce pain and swelling.

Step 4. Elevate: Keep the injured area elevated to help reduce swelling.

Step 5. Rest: Encourage the patient to rest and avoid putting weight on the injured joint.

Step 6. Compression: Apply a compression bandage if available to help reduce swelling and provide support.

Step 7. Seek Medical Attention: Visit a healthcare professional as soon as possible for proper diagnosis and treatment. They can safely relocate the joint and provide further care.

Note-


  • • Avoid Movement: Moving the injured joint can worsen the injury and delay healing.
  • • Immobilize: Splinting the joint helps prevent further damage and promotes proper healing.
  • • Apply Ice: Cold therapy reduces pain and swelling by constricting blood vessels.
  • • Elevate: Elevating the injured area above the heart helps reduce swelling by promoting drainage.
  • • Rest: Resting the injured joint allows tissues to heal and prevents further strain.
  • • Compression: Compression bandages help control swelling and provide stability to the injured joint.
  • • Seek Medical Attention: Professional evaluation is essential to ensure proper treatment and prevent complications.
