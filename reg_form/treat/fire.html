Based on the symptoms you've provided, the first aid treatment is as follows:

Step 1. Stop, Drop, and Roll: If you're on fire, stop, drop to the ground, and roll to put out the flames.

Step 2. Cool the Burn: Run cool water over the burned area for 10-20 minutes. It helps ease pain and swelling.

Step 3. Cover the Burn: Once cooled, cover the burn with a clean cloth or bandage to protect it.

Step 4. Seek Medical Help: Even if it seems small, get medical help, especially for big burns or burns on the face, hands, feet, or private parts.

Step 5. Don't Apply Home Remedies: Avoid putting butter, oil, or anything else on the burn. It can make things worse.

Step 6. Keep Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated.

Step 7. Watch for Signs of Infection: Look for increased pain, redness, swelling, or pus. If you see any, go back to the doctor.

Step 8. Follow Doctor's Advice: Do what the doctor says for taking care of the burn.

Note-


  • • Stop, Drop, and Roll: Immediate action can prevent further injury from fire.
  • • Cool the Burn: Cooling the burn with water helps reduce pain and swelling.
  • • Cover the Burn: Protecting the burn with a clean cloth or bandage helps prevent infection.
  • • Seek Medical Help: Professional evaluation is crucial, especially for severe burns or those in sensitive areas.
  • • Avoid Home Remedies: Certain substances can worsen burns or increase the risk of infection.
  • • Stay Hydrated: Drinking water supports the body's healing process.
  • • Monitor for Infection: Promptly addressing signs of infection helps prevent complications.
  • • Adhere to Medical Advice: Following the doctor's recommendations promotes proper healing and reduces the risk of complications.
