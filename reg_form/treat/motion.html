Based on the symptoms you've provided, the first aid treatment is as follows:

Step 1. Avoid further movement: Limit any movements that exacerbate the pain or discomfort to prevent further injury.

Step 2. Apply ice: Use ice packs wrapped in a cloth to reduce swelling and alleviate pain. Apply for 15-20 minutes every few hours.

Step 3. Immobilize the area: If possible, stabilize the affected area with a splint or sling to prevent further damage and promote healing.

Step 4. Seek medical attention: If the restricted range of motion is severe or persists despite initial measures, seek emergency medical care promptly.

Step 5. Do not attempt to force movement: Trying to forcefully move a joint with restricted motion can worsen the injury or condition. It's important to wait for proper evaluation and treatment by a healthcare professional.

Note-


  • • Avoid Further Movement: Preventing additional movement helps prevent further injury and promotes healing.
  • • Apply Ice: Cold therapy with ice packs reduces swelling and provides pain relief.
  • • Immobilize the Area: Stabilizing the affected area with a splint or sling prevents further damage.
  • • Seek Medical Attention: Severe or persistent restricted range of motion may require immediate medical evaluation and treatment.
  • • Do Not Force Movement: Forcing movement can exacerbate the injury and delay healing. Always seek professional medical advice.
