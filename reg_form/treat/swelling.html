Based on the symptoms you've provided, the first aid treatment is as follows:

Step 1. Rest and elevate the swollen area: Reduce movement and elevate the affected area above the heart to minimize swelling.

Step 2. Apply ice wrapped in a cloth: Use ice packs wrapped in a cloth to reduce swelling and alleviate pain.

Step 3. Use compression if possible: Apply compression with a bandage or wrap to help reduce swelling.

Step 4. Avoid activities that worsen swelling: Minimize activities that may exacerbate swelling or cause further injury.

Step 5. Drink plenty of water: Stay hydrated to support the body's natural healing process.

Step 6. Keep an eye on any changes in symptoms: Monitor the swelling for any changes or worsening of symptoms.

Step 7. Take over-the-counter pain relievers if needed: If discomfort is significant, consider taking over-the-counter pain medications as directed.

Step 8. Seek medical help if swelling is severe or accompanied by other symptoms: If swelling is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other concerning symptoms, seek medical attention promptly.

Note-


  • • Rest and Elevate: Reducing movement and elevating the affected area can help reduce swelling.
  • • Apply Ice: Cold therapy with ice packs can constrict blood vessels and reduce swelling.
  • • Use Compression: Compression helps limit swelling and provides support to the affected area.
  • • Avoid Aggravating Activities: Minimize activities that may worsen swelling or cause further injury.
  • • Stay Hydrated: Drinking water supports the body's natural healing process and helps reduce swelling.
  • • Monitor Symptoms: Keep an eye on any changes in swelling or accompanying symptoms.
  • • Use Pain Relievers: Over-the-counter pain medications can help alleviate discomfort associated with swelling.
  • • Seek Medical Attention: If swelling is severe, persistent, or accompanied by other concerning symptoms, seek medical help promptly.
